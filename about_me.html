About Me

                                        I am currently a resident at the department of pediatrics at Hacettepe University, 
                                        a tertiary referral hospital in Ankara, Turkey. I have been working with statistical 
                                        analysis and predictive modeling of biomedical data for about two years. As a medical doctor, 
                                        I like to produce solutions to problems in the field of medicine with artificial intelligence. 
                                        Machine learning models could be used to observe the vital signs of patients receiving critical 
                                        care and alert clinicians if certain risk factors increase. While medical devices like heart monitors 
                                        can track vital signs, AI can collect the data from those devices and look for more complex conditions, 
                                        such as sepsis or other critical conditions. These days, I am developing myself in special optimization algorithms,
                                        fuzzy logic, graph analysis, and time series algorithms in the analysis of biomedical information. Even more importantly,
                                        I am developing real-time analysis of stream biomedical data. In this way, we will have the chance to make an 
                                        early diagnosis or interventions with anomaly detection algorithms in patients whose condition is more critical patients.
